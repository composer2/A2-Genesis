JOB AND CAREER RESOURCES FOR TEENS

Are you a teenager looking for a job? Or perhaps a parent who is helping your kid to get their first job? Whether you are looking for a part-time job or internship, we have 169 companies in our database which may hire teenagers.

What you can do

  1. Start researching.Find organizations and other places that you know pay teens to work.
  2. Get the word out. You can post flyers in your neighborhood detailing jobs that you can do
  3. Use your connections. Talk to friends and family about any paying jobs they might know of, and follow through.
  4. Volunteer. Sometimes, the best way to get a paying job is to begin as a volunteer and work your way up
  5. Make a resume. Even if most jobs you apply for don't ask for a resume, it's a good exercise, and it's useful to have your work history and education details
  6. Apply early! The most important key to getting jobs with these places is to apply as soon as you hear about jobs
  7. Go to an interview. One of the most often asked questions is: Why do you feel you are qualified for this job?
  8. Increase your earnings. After working at the entry level position for a few summers or after school, you will the have the experience to work at a higher level
